Discrete Time Markov Chains

Definition. Notations

  • T={0,1,2,...}\mathcal{T}=\{0,1,2,...\}T={0,1,2,...}: the set of discrete time
  • SSS: a discrete set of states

A discrete time Markov chain is a sequence {Xn:n=0,1,2,...}\{X_n:n=0,1,2,...\}{Xn​:n=0,1,2,...} of RVs taking values in SSS, with XnX_nXn​ being the state at time n∈Tn\in\mathcal{T}n∈T, and

  • Time homogeneous transition probability

    Pij=P{Xn+1=j∣Xn=i},∀ i,j∈S,∀ n∈TP_{ij}=\mathbb{P}\{X_{n+1}=j\mid X_n=i\},\quad\forall\,i,j\in S,\quad\forall\,n\in\mathcal{T} Pij​=P{Xn+1​=j∣Xn​=i},∀i,j∈S,∀n∈T

  • Markov Property: ∀ n≥1\forall\,n\ge 1∀n≥1, ∀ i0,i1,...,in−1,i,j∈S\forall\,i_0,i_1,...,i_{n-1},i,j\in S∀i0​,i1​,...,in−1​,i,j∈S,

    P{Xn+1=j∣X0=i0, X1=i1, ...,Xn−1=in−1, Xn=i}=Pij=P{Xn+1=j∣Xn=i} .\mathbb{P}\{X_{n+1}=j\mid X_0=i_0,\,X_1=i_1,\,...,X_{n-1}=i_{n-1},\,X_n=i\}=P_{ij}=\mathbb{P}\{X_{n+1}=j\mid X_n=i\}\,. P{Xn+1​=j∣X0​=i0​,X1​=i1​,...,Xn−1​=in−1​,Xn​=i}=Pij​=P{Xn+1​=j∣Xn​=i}.

Interpretation of the Markov Property. In plain words, the Markov Property says that given the current state, the past information is irrelevant to determining the future information.

  • Past states: X0=i0,X1=i1,...,Xn−1=in−1X_0=i_0, X_1=i_1,...,X_{n-1}=i_{n-1}X0​=i0​,X1​=i1​,...,Xn−1​=in−1​
  • Current sate: Xn=iX_n=iXn​=i
  • Future state: Xn+1=jX_{n+1}=jXn+1​=j

Construction of DTMCs

Theorem. Suppose {Xn}\{X_n\}{Xn​} is a sequence of RVs satisfying Xn+1=f(Xn,Un+1)X_{n+1}=f(X_n,U_{n+1})Xn+1​=f(Xn​,Un+1​), where

  • f:S×U→Sf:S\times\mathcal{U}\to Sf:S×U→S
  • {Un}\{U_n\}{Un​} is a sequence of iid RVs.

Then {Xn}\{X_n\}{Xn​} is a DTMC with state space SSS.

Proof. We need to show that {Xn}\{X_n\}{Xn​} has time homogeneous transition probability and satisfies the Markov Property.

∀ n∈T\forall\,n\in\mathcal{T}∀n∈T and ∀ i,j∈S\forall\,i,j\in S∀i,j∈S,

\begin{align} &\qquad \mathbb{P}(X_{n+1}=j\mid X_0=i_0,\,X_1=i_1,\,...,\,X_{n-1}=i_{n-1},\,X_n=i) \\ &= \mathbb{P}(f(X_n,U_{n+1})=j\mid X_0=i_0,\,X_1=i_1,\,...,\,X_{n-1}=i_{n-1},\,X_n=i)\,. \end{align}

Now

f(Xn,Un+1)=f(f(Xn−1,Un),Un+1)=⋯=f(f(⋯f(f(X0,U1),U2)⋯ ,Un),Un+1)f(X_n,U_{n+1})=f(f(X_{n-1},U_n),U_{n+1})=\cdots=f(f(\cdots f(f(X_0,U_1),U_2)\cdots,U_{n}), U_{n+1}) f(Xn​,Un+1​)=f(f(Xn−1​,Un​),Un+1​)=⋯=f(f(⋯f(f(X0​,U1​),U2​)⋯,Un​),Un+1​)

depends on X0,U1,U2,...,Un+1X_0,U_1,U_2,...,U_{n+1}X0​,U1​,U2​,...,Un+1​. Therefore
