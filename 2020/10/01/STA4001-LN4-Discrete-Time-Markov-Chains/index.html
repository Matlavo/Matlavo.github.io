STA4001 LN4: Discrete Time Markov Chains

Discrete Time Markov Chains

Definition. Notations

  • $\mathcal{T}={0,1,2,…}$: the set of discrete time
  • $S$: a discrete set of states

A discrete time Markov chain is a sequence ${X_n:n=0,1,2,…}$ of RVs taking values in $S$, with $X_n$ being the state at time $n\in\mathcal{T}$, and

  • Time homogeneous transition probability
    $$
    P_{ij}=\mathbb{P}{X_{n+1}=j\mid X_n=i},\quad\forall,i,j\in S,\quad\forall,n\in\mathcal{T}
    $$

  • Markov Property: $\forall,n\ge 1$, $\forall,i_0,i_1,…,i_{n-1},i,j\in S$,
    $$
    \mathbb{P}{X_{n+1}=j\mid X_0=i_0,,X_1=i_1,,…,X_{n-1}=i_{n-1},,X_n=i}=P_{ij}=\mathbb{P}{X_{n+1}=j\mid X_n=i},.
    $$

Interpretation of the Markov Property. In plain words, the Markov Property says that given the current state, the past information is irrelevant to determining the future information.

  • Past states: $X_0=i_0, X_1=i_1,…,X_{n-1}=i_{n-1}$
  • Current sate: $X_n=i$
  • Future state: $X_{n+1}=j$

Construction of DTMCs

Theorem. Suppose ${X_n}$ is a sequence of RVs satisfying $X_{n+1}=f(X_n,U_{n+1})$, where

  • $f:S\times\mathcal{U}\to S$
  • ${U_n}$ is a sequence of iid RVs.

Then ${X_n}$ is a DTMC with state space $S$.

Proof. We need to show that ${X_n}$ has time homogeneous transition probability and satisfies the Markov Property.

$\forall,n\in\mathcal{T}$ and $\forall,i,j\in S$,
$$
\begin{align}
&\qquad \mathbb{P}(X_{n+1}=j\mid X_0=i_0,,X_1=i_1,,…,,X_{n-1}=i_{n-1},,X_n=i) \
&=
\mathbb{P}(f(X_n,U_{n+1})=j\mid X_0=i_0,,X_1=i_1,,…,,X_{n-1}=i_{n-1},,X_n=i),.
\end{align}
$$

Now
$$
f(X_n,U_{n+1})=f(f(X_{n-1},U_n),U_{n+1})=\cdots=f(f(\cdots f(f(X_0,U_1),U_2)\cdots,U_{n}), U_{n+1})
$$
depends on $X_0,U_1,U_2,…,U_{n+1}$. Therefore
$$
\begin{align}
&\qquad\mathbb{P}(f(X_n,U_{n+1})=j\mid X_0=i_0,,X_1=i_1,,…,,X_{n-1}=i_{n-1},,X_n=i) \
&=
\mathbb{P}(f(X_n,U_{n+1})=j\mid X_0=i_0,,X_1=i_1,,…,,X_{n-1}=i_{n-1},,X_n=i) \
\end{align}
$$
