STA4001 LN8: Stationary Distribution, Recurrence Property

We have reached the core of MC theory: stationary distribution and recurrence property.

Gambler’s Ruin Problem

Consider a DTMC with state space S={0,1,2,3,4}S=\{0,1,2,3,4\}S={0,1,2,3,4} and transition probabilities P00=P44=1P_{00}=P_{44}=1P00​=P44​=1, Pi,i+1=0.2P_{i,i+1}=0.2Pi,i+1​=0.2, and Pi,i−1=0.8P_{i,i-1}=0.8Pi,i−1​=0.8 for i∈{1,2,3}i\in\{1,2,3\}i∈{1,2,3}. We call states 000 and 444 absorbing states.

Interpretation: A gambler enters a casino with 111 dollars. He wins 111 dollar with probability 0.20.20.2, and loses 111 dollar with probability 0.80.80.8. He plays repeatedly and stops playing when his balance reaches either 000 or 444 dollars.

Stationary Distribution

Previously we talked about transient behaviors; we studied finite-time behavior. Now we study what happens when t→∞t\to\inftyt→∞.

Definition. π={πi:i∈S}\pi=\{\pi_i:i\in S\}π={πi​:i∈S} is called a stationary distribution with state space SSS (SSS may be infinite) and transition matrix PPP if

  • ∑i∈Sπi=1\sum_{i\in S}\pi_i=1∑i∈S​πi​=1
  • π=πP\pi=\pi Pπ=πP (balance equation, π\piπ viewed as a probability row vector)
  • π≥0\pi\ge 0π≥0

Example. For P=(0.20.80.50.5)P=\begin{pmatrix}0.2&0.8\\0.5&0.5\end{pmatrix}P=(0.20.5​0.80.5​), we have π=(5/13,8/13)\pi=(5/13,8/13)π=(5/13,8/13).

Interpretation of balance equation. Suppose the initial state follows a distribution, say, X0∼πX_0\sim\piX0​∼π with P(X0=i)=πiP(X_0=i)=\pi_iP(X0​=i)=πi​. Then

P(X1=j)=∑iP(X0=i)P(X1=j∣X0=i)=∑iPi,jπi=(πP)j .P(X_1=j)=\sum_{i}P(X_0=i)P(X_1=j\mid X_0=i)=\sum_{i}P_{i,j}\pi_i=(\pi P)_j\,. P(X1​=j)=i∑​P(X0​=i)P(X1​=j∣X0​=i)=i∑​Pi,j​πi​=(πP)j​.

Hence X1∼πPX_1\sim\pi PX1​∼πP. More generally, Xn∼πPnX_n\sim\pi P^nXn​∼πPn.

Therefore if X0∼πX_0\sim \piX0​∼π with π\piπ being stationary, then Xn∼πX_n\sim \piXn​∼π for all nnn. This is why π\piπ is call stationary.

Irreducible DTMC

Definitions.

  • State iii reaches state jjj if there exists a path i=i0,i1,...,in−1,in=ji=i_0,i_1,...,i_{n-1},i_n=ji=i0​,i1​,...,in−1​,in​=j such that Pik,ik+1>0P_{i_k,i_{k+1}}>0Pik​,ik+1​​>0 for all kkk.
  • A DTMC is called irreducible if every pair of states reach each other.
  • iii and jjj communicates if they reach each other.

Remark. Geometrically, an irreducible DTMC is a connected graph.

Remark. iii reaches jjj ⇏\not\Rightarrow​⇒ jjj reaches iii.

Lemma.

  • State iii reaches jjj if there exists an integer n≥1n\ge 1n≥1 if (Pn)ij>0(P^n)_{ij}>0(Pn)ij​>0.
  • States i→ji\to ji→j and j→kj\to kj→k imply that i→ki\to ki→k.
