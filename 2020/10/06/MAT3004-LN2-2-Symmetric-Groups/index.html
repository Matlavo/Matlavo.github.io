Symmetric Groups

Definition. Let XXX be a set. A permutation of XXX is a bijection σ:X→X\sigma:X\to Xσ:X→X.

Notice that the structure of a group Sym(X)\text{Sym}(X)Sym(X) is concerned only with the number of elements in XXX, not what the elements in XXX are. If AAA and BBB have the same cardinality, then Sym(A)≅Sym(B)\text{Sym}(A)\cong\text{Sym}(B)Sym(A)≅Sym(B). Decompositions of Permutations

Theorem. Each σ∈Sn\sigma\in S_nσ∈Sn​ is a product of disjoint cycles.

We will prove this theorem later with the tools from the theory equivalence class.

Definition. A transposition is a 222-cycle.

Theorem. Every cycle can be written as a product of transpositions.

Proof. (i1,i2,...,in)=(i1,in)⋯(i1,i3)(i1,i2)(i_1,i_2,...,i_n)=(i_1,i_n)\cdots(i_1,i_3)(i_1,i_2)(i1​,i2​,...,in​)=(i1​,in​)⋯(i1​,i3​)(i1​,i2​). Q.E.D.

Corollary. Every permutation is a product of transpositions.

Intuitively, this corollary just states that any rearrangement of nnn objects can be achieved by successively interchanging pairs of them.

Note, however, that the transpositions may not be disjoint, and a representation of a permutation in this way is not unique. For example, we cam always insert at the beginning the transposition (1,2)(1,2)(1,2) twice, for (1,2)(1,2)(1,2)(1,2)(1,2)(1,2) is the identity permutation. What is true is that the number of transpositions used to represent a given permutation must either always be even or always be odd.

Theorem. In every factorization of σ\sigmaσ as a product of transpositions, the number of factors is always even or odd.

Proof. Think of elements in SnS_nSn​ as permutations of the nnn rows of the n×nn\times nn×n identity matrix InI_nIn​. The identity matrix has determinant 111, and interchanging any two rows changes the sign of the determinant. Let AσA_{\sigma}Aσ​ be the matrix obtained by a permutation σ\sigmaσ of rows of InI_nIn​. If AσA_{\sigma}Aσ​ could be obtain from InI_nIn​ by both an even number and an odd number of transpositions of rows, its determinant would have to be 111 and −1-1−1, which is impossible. This yields the desired conclusion. Q.E.D.

Remark. Note that if σ\sigmaσ is a transposition, then det⁡(Aσ)=−1\det(A_{\sigma})=-1det(Aσ​)=−1.

Definition. A permutation of a finite set is even or odd according to whether it can be expressed as a product of an even number of transpositions or an odd number transpositions, respectively.
