Generating Sets

Definition. Let GGG be a group and SSS a nonempty subset of GGG. The set

⟨S⟩={a1m1a2m2⋯anmn∣n∈N,ai∈S,mi∈Z}\langle S\rangle=\{a_1^{m_1}a_2^{m_2}\cdots a_n^{m_n}\mid n\in\mathbb{N},a_i\in S,m_i\in\mathbb{Z}\} ⟨S⟩={a1m1​​a2m2​​⋯anmn​​∣n∈N,ai​∈S,mi​∈Z}

is a subgroup of GGG and is called the subgroup of GGG generated by SSS. If G=⟨S⟩G=\langle S\rangleG=⟨S⟩, then we say that SSS is a generating set for GGG.

Note that in the definition of ⟨S⟩\langle S\rangle⟨S⟩, the aia_iai​'s need not be distinct.

Zp1r1×⋯×Zpmrm×Zn\mathbb{Z}_{p_1^{r_1}}\times\cdots\times\mathbb{Z}_{p_m^{r_m}}\times\mathbb{Z}^n Zp1r1​​​×⋯×Zpmrm​​​×Zn
