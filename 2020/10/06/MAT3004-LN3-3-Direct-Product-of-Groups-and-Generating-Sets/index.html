Theorem. The group $\mathbb{Z}m\times\mathbb{Z}_n$ is cyclic (and is thus isomorphic to $\mathbb{Z}{mn}$) if and only if $\gcd(m,n)=1$.

Proof. $(\Leftarrow)$ If $\gcd(m,n)=1$, we will show that $|\langle(1,1)\rangle|=|(1,1)|=mn$. Suppose $k=|(1,1)|$. Then $k(1,1)=(k1,k1)=(0,0)$ implies $k=xm$ and $k=ym$ for some $x,y\in\mathbb{Z}^+$. Thus $k=\text{lcm}(m,n)=mn/\gcd(m,n)=mn$. We conclude that $|\langle(1,1)\rangle|=|(1,1)|=mn$. Example. Find all abelian groups of order $360$ up to isomorphism.

Solution. Since the group is of finite order, then no factor of $\mathbb{Z}$ will appear. We factor $360=2^33^25$. Then for each of the prime powers, we list all the possibilities:

  • $2^3$: $\mathbb{Z}2^3$(with subscripts corresponding to $2\times 2\times 2$), $\mathbb{Z}{2^2}\times\mathbb{Z}2$(corresponding to $2^2\times 2$), $\mathbb{Z}{2^3}$ (corresponding to $2^3$)
  • $3^2$: $\mathbb{Z}3^2$, $\mathbb{Z}{3^2}$
  • $5$: $\mathbb{Z}_5$

Thus all the possibilities are

  1. $\mathbb{Z}_2^3\times\mathbb{Z}_3^2\times\mathbb{Z}_5$
  2. $\mathbb{Z}2^3\times\mathbb{Z}{3^2}\times\mathbb{Z}_5$
  3. $\mathbb{Z}_{2^2}\times\mathbb{Z}_2\times\mathbb{Z}_3^2\times\mathbb{Z}_5$
  4. $\mathbb{Z}{2^2}\times\mathbb{Z}_2\times\mathbb{Z}{3^2}\times\mathbb{Z}_5$
  5. $\mathbb{Z}_{2^3}\times\mathbb{Z}_3^2\times\mathbb{Z}_5$
  6. $\mathbb{Z}{2^3}\times\mathbb{Z}{3^2}\times\mathbb{Z}_5$

Example. Find all abelian groups of order $7^5$ up to isomorphism.

Solution. Essentially we find all the factorizations of $7^5$.

  1. $7\times7\times7\times7\times 7\leadsto\mathbb{Z}_7^5$ (highest power is $1$)
  2. $7^2\times7\times7\times7\leadsto\mathbb{Z}_{7^2}\times\mathbb{Z}_7^3$ (highest power is $2$)
  3. $7^2\times7^2\times7\leadsto\mathbb{Z}_{7^2}^2\times\mathbb{Z}_7$ (highest power is $2$)
  4. $7^3\times 7\times 7\leadsto\mathbb{Z}_{7^3}\times\mathbb{Z}_7^2$ (highest power is $3$)
  5. $7^3\times 7^2\leadsto\mathbb{Z}{7^3}\times\mathbb{Z}{7^2}$ (highest power is $3$)
  6. $7^4\times 7\leadsto\mathbb{Z}_{7^4}\times\mathbb{Z}_7$ (highest power is $4$)
  7. $7^5\leadsto\mathbb{Z}_{7^5}$ (highest power is $5$)
