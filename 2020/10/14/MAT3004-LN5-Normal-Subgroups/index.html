MAT3004 LN5 Normal Subgroups

Definition. A subgroup $H\le G$ is normal if $aH=Ha\,\forall\,a\in G$. We write $H\unlhd G$. If $H<G$, then we write $H\lhd G$.

Theorem. TFAE.

  • $H\unlhd G$
  • $g^{-1}Hg\subset H\,\forall g\in G$ ($\Leftrightarrow g^{-1}hg\in H\ \forall\,g\in G,\ \forall\,h\in H$)
  • $g^{-1}Hg=H\,\forall\,g\in G$

Proof. $(1)\Rightarrow(2)$ Suppose $g\in G$. $gH=Hg$ implies $gHg^{-1}=H$. Thus $g^{-1}Hg\subset H$.

$(2)\Rightarrow (3)$ $g^{-1}Hg\subset H\Rightarrow H\subset gHg^{-1}$. Replacing $g$ by $g^{-1}$ yields $H\subset g^{-1}Hg$. Thus $H=g^{-1}Hg$.

$(3)\Rightarrow (1)$ Obvious. Pre-multiply $g$ on both sides. Q.E.D.

Therefore to check that a subgroup is normal, it suffices to verify (2).

  • $(ij)(jk)=-ik=ki$.

  • $(ii)j(kk)=j=-ik$, so $ji=j$.

Hence $ij=k$ and $jk=i$. This is called the triality of $Q_8$.

Definition. If every subgroup of $G$ is normal, then $G$ is called Dedekind. Then every abelian group is Dedekind. If $G$ is non-abelian and Dedekind, then we say $G$ is Hamiltonian.

Derived Subgroup

Definition. Let $a,b\in G$. The commutator of $a$ and $b$ is $[a,b]=a^{-1}b^{-1}ab$. The derived subgroup of $G$ is

Remark. Some elements in $G’$ may NOT be commutators.

Theorem. $G’$ is normal.

Proof. For all $g\in G$, for all $h\in G’$, $g^{-1}hg=g^{-1}hgh^{-1}h=[g,h^{-1}]h\in G’$. Q.E.D.

The following two theorems say that the derived subgroup $G’$ is the smallest normal subgroup $H\unlhd G$ such that $G/H$ is abelian.

Theorem. $G/G’$ is abelian.

Proof. $\forall\,a,b\in G$, $a^{-1}b^{-1}ab\in G’$, so $ab\in (ba)G’$. Since $ab\in (ab)G$, then $(bG’)(aG’)=(ba)G’=(ab)G’=(aG’)(bG’)$. Q.E.D.

Theorem. Let $H\unlhd G$. Then $G/H$ is abelian if and only if $G’\le H$.

Proof. $\Rightarrow)$ $\forall\,a,b\in H$, $(ab)H=(ba)H$, so $a^{-1}b^{-1}abH=H$. Hence $a^{-1}b^{-1}abe^{-1}=a^{-1}b^{-1}ab\in H$. So $H\ge G’$.

$\Leftarrow)$ $\forall\,a,b\in H$, $a^{-1}b^{-1}ab\in G’\le H$, so $(a^{-1}b^{-1}ab)H=H$. Hence $(ab)H=(ba)H$. Q.E.D.
